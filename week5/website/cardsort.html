Card Sorting Exercise: My Findings

After finishing this exercise, I found that there were lots of ways that the participants saw as to how best to organize the information. It was common for the participants to categorize the "News Feed" with "Twitter" and to put the Past, Present and Future Missions in the same category, but most of the other information hierarchies that were created were quiet different. Still all the users had a organizational structure that was entirely different from the one that I had made, and it is understandable why there is a need to have multiple participants test evaluate the information hierarchy of a site.

Card Sorting Data for NASA Moon Site: Erin

This participant was unique compared to the other participants, because she had more categories other than the other participants. The participant organized the cards into four sections and tried to view the heirarchy as more of a website. The first sections was the header of the website and would follow with every page. the Second section was the sidebar and had relative navigation information. The fourth section had footer content and the third section would contain content. The participant also demonstrated the layout of the site which is illustrated in the picture above. Out of all the participants, this one got closest to the actually information hierarchy of the NASA site, and was the only one to think about the possible layout of the information on the page.
